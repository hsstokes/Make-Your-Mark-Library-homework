Step 1 — Find Your Research Theme

Your marks should express your research idea. Ask yourself:

  • What connects your artist references?
  • Is there a feeling or texture in your research images?
  • If your project had a material quality — rough, fragile, geometric — what would it be?

💡 Important question

Does this mark feel like my research theme? A mark about decay should feel different from a mark about structure.

Step 2 — Plan Your Mark Set

You need at least 8–10 marks. Plan one mark for each function:

→ 1–2 arrows Connect images together
╱ 1–2 dividers Create zones on the page
◻ 1 frame Highlight part of an image
≈ 2–3 textures Hatching, stipple, grain
✦ 1 emphasis Flag a key reference
∿ 1 connector Link two ideas together

Step 3 — Download the Calligraphr Template

Getting Your Template
1
Log in to Calligraphr

Go to calligraphr.com — use your Session 1 account.

2
Download the Punctuation Template
  • Click Templates
  • Select the punctuation / special characters set
  • Download as PDF — print on white paper
3
Plan Your Mark Map

Write a small pencil note at the top of each box — which mark goes here? Keep this note — you will need it in class.

Step 4 — Make Your Marks

Use black ink on the printed template. Fill each box boldly.

Example — Completed Mark Template
[mark-template-example.jpg — add to repository]

✓ Works well

  • Bold brush marks in black ink
  • Thick pen gestures
  • Stamp impressions
  • Drawn shapes — arrows, rectangles
  • Flicked or splattered ink

⚠ Test first

  • Very light pencil
  • Pale or diluted ink
  • Very fine crosshatching
  • Photographic textures
Important: Calligraphr traces whatever black shape it finds. Keep marks dark, clear, and inside the box.

💡 Tips

  • Make 2–3 versions of each mark and choose the best
  • Wait for ink to dry before scanning
  • Make all marks in one session so they feel consistent

Step 5 — Scan Your Template

  • Scan at 300 DPI or higher
  • Or use Adobe Scan or Microsoft Lens on your phone
  • Save as JPEG
  • Check it is straight — no shadows across the boxes
Bring to class: your scanned JPEG and your mark map (the note showing which mark is in which box).
